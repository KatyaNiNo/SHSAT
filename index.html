Anatomy of a Linear Function

You already know the formula. Now learn what every single piece actually means.

y = mx + b
y
TotalOutput

y is always your total. Whatever the problem asks you to find at the end, that is y. It lives in the same unit as the top of the fridge.

m
SlopeRateAlways a Fraction

m is your rate. It always has two units: miles per hour, tickets per dollar. It is never just one number by itself.

x
InputBottom of Fridge

x represents the quantity that matches the bottom unit of your slope. Slope is miles per hour? x is the number of hours.

b
y-interceptStarting PointInitial Value

b is where you start before anything happens. It shares the same unit as y (top of the fridge).

The Fridge

The slope m is always a fraction with two units. Think of it as a fridge: the top shelf is the numerator unit, the bottom shelf is the denominator unit. Once you set up the fridge, the units for every other variable fall into place automatically.

Top shelf
miles
Bottom shelf
hour
→
Top of fridge = numerator unit of slope (miles). This is also the unit of y and b.
Bottom of fridge = denominator unit (hour). This is the unit of x.
y and b both share the top-of-fridge unit. Both are in miles.
Fridge Rule in Action
m = miles/hour → x = hours → y = miles + b = miles
m = tickets/dollar → x = dollars → y = tickets + b = tickets
m = points/mistake → x = mistakes → y = points + b = points

How to Attack Any Word Problem

Find the rate first. Look for "per" or "each." That is your slope m. Put both units in the fridge.

Find the starting value. Look for "flat fee," "already," "starts with." That is b, in the top-of-fridge unit.

The bottom of the fridge tells you what x counts.

y is always the total the question is asking you to find, in the top-of-fridge unit.

No starting value mentioned? b = 0. The line passes through the origin.
